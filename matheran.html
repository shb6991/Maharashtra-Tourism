Matheran (Raigad)




  • Matheran, is Asias tiniest hill station and remains pollution free because no motorized vehicles are allowed on its tiny roads or the pathways that lead into deep woods.

  • It has lots to offer by way of scenic view points, long treks through forested areas, and a quaintness of its own.

  • The best part about going to Matheran, the starting point for which is the railway station of Neral on the Mumbai-Pune route, is that you can buy a ride on the tiny train that traverses the narrow gauge track to the top, offering you on the way breathtaking sights even as it negotiates the bends and the curves.

  • Known as the ‘pedestrian hill station because all vehicles have to per force stop at Dasturi and tourists have to either walk or hitch a horse ride into Matheran, it is perched at 803 meters above the sea level.

  • Spread across Matheran is a mix of homes and hotels – some still retaining their old-world charm of spacious bungalows while the others offering the most modern conveniences..

    • How to reach?

    Air:
    Nearest airport is at Mumbai, 110 kms away.

    Rail:
    Mini train 21 kms from Neral (two hours). Check the train services time schedules as they are suspended during the monsoon..

    Road:
    From Neral, a taxi can be hired. The distance is 8 kms..
